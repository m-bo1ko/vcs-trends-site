History of Version Control Systems

The history of version control began in the 1970s with local systems like SCCS and RCS. These tools allowed developers to keep track of code changes but only worked on a single machine.

In the 1990s, centralized systems such as CVS and later Subversion (SVN) appeared, allowing multiple users to collaborate through a central server. However, these systems suffered from a single point of failure and limited offline capabilities.

The major shift came with distributed systems such as Git and Mercurial in the mid-2000s. They enabled each developer to have a full copy of the repository, improving speed, reliability, and flexibility.
